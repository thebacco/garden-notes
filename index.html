Fertilizer Chart

Compost / worm castings Base soil food

Use for: Most vegetables, seedlings, containers, tired soil.

When: Before planting, at transplanting, or as a light side-dress.

How: Mix compost into the bed. Scratch worm castings into the top soil and water in.

Fish emulsion Nitrogen / leafy growth

Use for: Kale, lettuce, young plants, pale leaves, slow leafy growth.

When: Every 2 to 4 weeks during active leaf growth.

How: Dilute with water. Use half-strength for tender plants. Stop going heavy once fruiting starts.

Kelp Stress support

Use for: Transplants, peppers, tomatoes, heat-stressed plants.

When: After transplanting, during heat swings, or when plants need recovery support.

How: Dilute liquid kelp or lightly apply meal. Treat it as support, not the main food.

Tomato / vegetable fertilizer Flowers and fruit

Use for: Tomatoes, peppers, cucumbers, zucchini, other heavy fruiting crops.

When: At first flowers or fruit set, then every 2 to 3 weeks while producing.

How: Apply at label rate around the drip line and water in. Avoid piling it against stems.

My Fertilizers

Tomato booster | 3-8-7

Farmer's Secret Tomato Booster

Use on
Tomatoes first. Also reasonable for fruiting peppers once they are flowering.
When
Weekly during the growing season, especially once plants are setting flowers and fruit.
How
Mix 2 tsp per gallon of water. Apply as a soil drench around the root zone.
Don't
Do not pour concentrate directly on plants. Do not use as the same-day heavy feed with Tiger Bloom.
Nitrogen | 5-1-1

Alaska Fish Fertilizer QT

Use on
Leafy greens, young plants, vegetables needing leaf growth, and pale/stalled plants.
When
Every 3 weeks during active growth. Use less once tomatoes and peppers start fruiting.
How
Shake well. For vegetables, mix 2 Tbsp per gallon of water over about 25 sq ft of soil. For root vegetables, use 1 Tbsp per gallon.
Don't
Do not store mixed solution. Avoid using it as the main feed once fruiting crops need bloom support.
Slow organic | 3-4-6

Espoma Tomato-tone

Use on
Tomatoes, peppers, squash, melons, and other fruiting crops.
When
At planting, again 10-14 days after plants establish, then twice monthly May through August.
How
Single plants: mix 3 Tbsp into soil at planting. Feeding: apply 3 Tbsp per plant in a narrow band at least 3 inches from the stem, then water thoroughly.
Don't
Do not pile it against the stem. It is dry food, so water it in after applying.
Bloom liquid | 2-8-4

FoxFarm Tiger Bloom

Use on
Fruiting and flowering plants that need bloom/fruit support, especially once flowers appear.
When
From first signs of flowering through harvest. General use is every other watering.
How
General feeding: mix 2-3 tsp per gallon of water. Heavy feeding: 4 tsp per gallon. For foliar feeding, use 1/2 tsp per gallon every other week.
Don't
Do not foliar spray in direct light or heat. Avoid stacking it with another bloom booster on the same watering.

Quick Summary

Base Compost and worm castings

Use before planting or as a light side-dress to support soil structure.

Growth Fish emulsion or blood meal

Nitrogen-forward feeds for leafy growth. Go easy once fruiting starts.

Fruit Tomato/vegetable fertilizer

Balanced or bloom-leaning blends for tomatoes, peppers, and cucumbers.

Stress Kelp meal or liquid kelp

Useful around transplanting, heat stress, and recovery weeks.
