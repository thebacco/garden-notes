Definitions

Fruit-Fill Crops
A practical feeding group for crops already carrying harvestable fruit. The goal is not more foliage; it is carbohydrate movement, potassium-supported fruit quality, steady water, and calcium transport in tomato/eggplant fruit.
Solanaceous Fruit
Fruit from Solanaceae crops such as tomato and eggplant. These crops can show blossom-end rot when calcium movement into rapidly expanding fruit is disrupted, often by irregular water uptake rather than absent soil calcium.
Cucurbits
Members of Cucurbitaceae, including zucchini and squash. Summer squash are commonly monoecious: separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Fruit set depends on female flower production, pollen transfer, water status, and harvest pressure.
Greens & Stalks
Vegetative crops where the eaten part is leaf, petiole, or grass-family tissue. Nitrogen and water drive usable growth, but calcium transport and even moisture still matter, especially for celery blackheart.
Roots & Storage Organs
Crops grown for underground tissues. They usually benefit from steady baseline fertility rather than repeated liquid pushes, because excessive nitrogen and moisture swings can reduce storage quality.

Fertilizer Timeline & Triggers

If leaves need pushing Use Alaska Fish

Celery, kale, or corn look pale, slow, dry-stressed, or hungry for foliage.

If vines slow down Use Tiger Bloom

Zucchini or squash are flowering poorly, dropping blossoms, or production is fading.

If fruit needs finishing Use Farmer's Secret

Tomatoes, eggplant, or strawberries are in late-season fruit fill and ripening mode.

If base feed is due Use Tomato-tone

Containers need the mid-season slow-release top-dress and calcium support.

If roots are fed Do nothing

Leeks and horseradish get one light Tomato-tone feed, then leave them alone.

Now

Base top-dress

Use: Espoma Tomato-tone

Feed: Tomatoes, zucchini, squash, eggplant, strawberries, leeks, horseradish.

Mix: 3 Tbsp per large container plant. Use 1.5 Tbsp for strawberries, leeks, and horseradish.

How: Pull mulch back, sprinkle around drip line, keep off stems, scratch into top inch, water deeply.

Every Sunday

Greens + corn jug

Use: Alaska Fish Fertilizer QT

Feed: Celery, kale, corn.

Mix: 4 Tbsp into a 2-gallon watering jug.

How: Pour at plant bases. Give celery enough volume that the root zone stays evenly moist.

Alternate Saturdays

Squash blossom surge

Use: FoxFarm Tiger Bloom

Feed: Zucchini and squash.

Mix: 4 tsp into a 2-gallon watering jug.

How: Apply during morning watering every 14 days to keep flower buds setting.

Mid-August onward

Tomato finish feed

Use: Farmer's Secret Tomato Booster

Feed: Tomatoes, eggplant, strawberries.

Mix: 4 tsp into a 2-gallon watering jug.

How: Apply every 10-14 days through late September for fruit fill, ripening, and flavor.

After base feed

Leave these alone

Feed: Leeks and horseradish.

Rule: One light Tomato-tone top-dress now, then no more feeding.

Why: Overfeeding can push soft growth, splitting, or root problems.

Product Reference

Tomato booster | 3-8-7 Tomatoes, eggplant, strawberries

Farmer's Secret Tomato Booster

Use on
Tomatoes, eggplant, and strawberries during late-season fruit fill.
When
Starting mid-August, every 10-14 days through late September.
How
Mix 4 tsp into a 2-gallon watering jug. Apply as a soil drench around the root zone.
Don't
Do not pour concentrate directly on plants. Do not use as the same-day heavy feed with Tiger Bloom.
Nitrogen | 5-1-1 Celery, kale, corn

Alaska Fish Fertilizer QT

Use on
Celery, kale, and corn when they need steady nitrogen and moisture.
When
Every Sunday during active growth.
How
Shake well. Mix 4 Tbsp into a 2-gallon watering jug and pour at plant bases.
Don't
Do not store mixed solution. Avoid using it as the main feed once fruiting crops need bloom support.
Slow organic | 3-4-6 Tomatoes, squash, eggplant, strawberries, leeks

Espoma Tomato-tone

Use on
Tomatoes, zucchini, squash, eggplant, strawberries, leeks, and horseradish as the base feed.
When
Now as a one-time mid-season top-dress.
How
Use 3 Tbsp per large container plant, or 1.5 Tbsp for strawberries, leeks, and horseradish. Keep at least 3 inches from stems and water thoroughly.
Don't
Do not pile it against the stem. It is dry food, so water it in after applying.
Bloom liquid | 2-8-4 Zucchini, squash

FoxFarm Tiger Bloom

Use on
Zucchini and squash that need steady flower and bud production.
When
Alternate Saturdays, every 14 days, during morning watering.
How
Mix 4 tsp into a 2-gallon watering jug and apply at the base.
Don't
Do not foliar spray in direct light or heat. Avoid stacking it with another bloom booster on the same watering.

Quick Summary

Base Compost and worm castings

Use before planting or as a light side-dress to support soil structure.

Growth Fish emulsion or blood meal

Nitrogen-forward feeds for leafy growth. Go easy once fruiting starts.

Fruit Tomato/vegetable fertilizer

Balanced or bloom-leaning blends for tomatoes, peppers, and cucumbers.

Stress Kelp meal or liquid kelp

Useful around transplanting, heat stress, and recovery weeks.
